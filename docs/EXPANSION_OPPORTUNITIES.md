# Expansion Opportunities

Last updated: June 4, 2026

## Framing

PromptPlay should keep a sharp core:

`durable YouTube learning paths`

But that does not mean the product has to stay narrow forever.

The right way to expand is:

1. keep one clear wedge
2. add adjacent surfaces that reuse the same data and trust layer
3. only expand into new formats or agent behaviors when they strengthen the core

The main shared asset across every expansion is:

`a structured, searchable, updateable graph of videos, paths, notes, summaries, progress, and user intent`

That asset can support several product lines.

## Expansion map

### Core product line: Learning OS

This remains the main product.

Core features:

- playlist import / generation
- module-based learning paths
- sync and repair
- notes and summaries
- flashcards, quizzes, review
- public and private sharing

Why it matters:

- strongest wedge
- clearest monetization
- highest compounding value

## Adjacent product lines

### 1. Research OS

Turn PromptPlay into a serious research workspace for people who use YouTube as a source of market, technical, or creative intelligence.

Use cases:

- AI founders tracking model releases, demos, and technical breakdowns
- creators tracking competitor channels
- marketers tracking industry analysis videos
- researchers building topic dossiers from talks and interviews

Feature ideas:

- research collections instead of only learning paths
- cross-video insight extraction
- claim / quote capture with timestamps
- compare channels or playlists on the same topic
- "what changed this week" digests
- export a research brief to Notion, Google Docs, or Markdown

Why this is attractive:

- higher willingness to pay than casual playlist tools
- stronger B2B potential
- makes PromptPlay useful even when the user is not "studying"

### 2. Creator Intelligence and Curation Studio

Serve creators, newsletter writers, and curators who package knowledge for others.

Use cases:

- creator resource hubs
- curated "best videos to learn X"
- creator ops teams tracking competitor content gaps
- paid resource libraries or communities

Feature ideas:

- branded public path pages
- path publishing templates
- fork/remix lineage
- channel gap detection
- topic coverage maps
- creator analytics for which paths get saved, remixed, completed

Why this is attractive:

- natural growth loop
- strong SEO surface
- monetizable as both SaaS and marketplace later

### 3. Team Enablement and Onboarding

Expand the product into company and cohort workflows.

Use cases:

- startup onboarding
- sales enablement
- customer education
- bootcamp / cohort assignments
- internal "learn this stack" paths

Feature ideas:

- workspaces
- assigned paths
- due dates
- manager analytics
- private comments and annotations
- branded private portals

Why this is attractive:

- clean B2B pricing
- high retention if embedded in workflow
- easier to justify sync, reliability, and admin investment

### 4. Coach and Community Product

Serve mentors, niche communities, and cohort leaders.

Use cases:

- Discord communities
- paid learning groups
- mentorship programs
- exam prep cohorts

Feature ideas:

- cohort paths
- weekly check-ins
- accountability reminders
- shared notes and discussion threads
- office-hour summaries
- student progress views

Why this is attractive:

- social accountability improves retention
- creator and coach segments can drive organic distribution

### 5. Certification and Interview Prep

Make the product more outcome-driven around specific goals.

Use cases:

- software engineering interview prep
- AWS / cloud certifications
- product management interviews
- design portfolio prep
- AI engineering upskilling

Feature ideas:

- competency maps
- practice checkpoints
- "you are weak on X" recommendations
- time-boxed study plans
- mock quizzes and review loops
- interview or exam mode paths

Why this is attractive:

- urgent pain
- measurable outcomes
- higher willingness to pay

## Farther expansions

These are plausible, but should come later.

### 6. Multi-source knowledge paths

Go beyond YouTube to include:

- blog posts
- docs
- GitHub repos
- PDFs
- podcasts

Best use:

- technical research
- onboarding
- certification prep

Risk:

- very easy to lose focus and become a generic summarizer

Recommendation:

- expand only after YouTube retention is strong
- treat non-video sources as supporting context, not the new core

### 7. Project-based learning

Move from "watch and remember" to "watch and build."

Feature ideas:

- capstone project suggestions
- coding sandboxes
- checklist-based practice
- project submissions
- rubric scoring

Why it could win:

- much stronger outcomes
- better monetization than playlists alone

Risk:

- this materially increases product complexity

### 8. Personal AI learning companion

Make PromptPlay feel more alive.

Feature ideas:

- voice tutor
- daily learning planner
- motivational accountability check-ins
- personalized "what to watch next"
- review scheduling assistant

Why it is cool:

- sticky experience
- more differentiated UX

Risk:

- can become gimmicky if not connected to real path data and progress

## Cool, multi-faceted product modes

These modes make the product feel bigger without losing coherence.

### Mode A: Learn

Goal:

- master a topic

Primary objects:

- paths, modules, progress, review

### Mode B: Research

Goal:

- extract insights from a collection of videos

Primary objects:

- collections, notes, briefs, comparisons

### Mode C: Curate

Goal:

- publish the best resources for a topic or audience

Primary objects:

- public paths, remix lineage, creator pages

### Mode D: Assign

Goal:

- give a path to a team, client, or cohort

Primary objects:

- workspaces, assignments, analytics

This is the best way to make PromptPlay multi-faceted:

`one data model, multiple modes, different surfaces for different jobs`

## Multi-agent ideas that are actually useful

Multi-agent is only worth it when each agent owns a distinct task or artifact.

Good PromptPlay agents:

### Scout Agent

Finds candidate videos, new channels, and updates related to a topic.

### Curator Agent

Ranks, deduplicates, and sequences resources into a path.

### Repair Agent

Monitors saved paths, detects broken items, and proposes replacements.

### Coach Agent

Turns the path into study prompts, quizzes, and follow-ups.

### Research Agent

Builds a brief or digest from a video collection.

### Publisher Agent

Turns a saved path into a polished public page with metadata and share copy.

### Team Ops Agent

Tracks assignment completion, nudges learners, and summarizes manager-facing progress.

## High-potential "cool" features

These are interesting without being pure novelty:

### Auto-updating path health

"Your React 2026 path lost 2 videos; here are 3 replacements and 1 better new module."

### Weekly intelligence brief

"Here is what changed across your saved AI engineering sources this week."

### Watch-to-build mode

For coding paths, PromptPlay creates a small task or scaffold after each module.

### Learning graph

Visualize how concepts, paths, channels, and notes connect.

### Benchmark me

Short assessment that estimates what to skip and what to focus on.

### Fork with rationale

Users can remix a public path and state why their version is better.

### Path battle / compare mode

Compare two paths for overlap, missing concepts, time cost, and freshness.

### Knowledge export

Push notes and summaries to Notion, Markdown, Anki, or Google Docs.

## Expansion ranking

### Best expansions now

1. Research OS
2. Creator / curation studio
3. Certification / interview prep
4. Team enablement

### Best expansions later

1. Multi-source knowledge paths
2. Project-based learning
3. Coach / community product
4. Voice companion

### Expansions to avoid early

1. becoming a generic all-content summarizer
2. becoming a social network
3. shipping flashy multi-agent UX before the underlying library is valuable
4. going mobile-first before extension and web retention are strong

## Best strategic interpretation

The product should become:

`a system for collecting, structuring, maintaining, and operationalizing long-form knowledge from YouTube`

Learning is the wedge.
Research is the best adjacent expansion.
Team workflows are the best monetization expansion.
Agentic experiences are the best "cool factor" expansion if they are grounded in durable product value.
